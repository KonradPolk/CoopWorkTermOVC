Co-op Work Term Report

Konrad Polkowski – Website Training & Support Specialist

Abstract / Introduction

During the Fall 2025 work term, I completed a four-month co-op placement as an OVC Website Training & Support Specialist Co-op Student at the University of Guelph’s Ontario Veterinary College (OVC). This role provided hands-on exposure to website support, faculty onboarding, and the use of enterprise tools within a large academic institution.

The purpose of this website is to reflect on the technical, professional, and communication skills I developed throughout my work term, as well as how these experiences contributed to my growth as a computing student preparing for future co-op and professional roles. This work term helped bridge the gap between academic coursework and real-world computing support in a complex, user-focused environment.

Information about the Employer

The Ontario Veterinary College (OVC) is one of Canada’s leading veterinary education and research institutions and is part of the University of Guelph, where I study. OVC supports a large and diverse community of faculty, staff, and students, with a strong focus on teaching, research, and clinical practice.

From a computing perspective, OVC makes extensive use of Microsoft 365 tools, particularly SharePoint, to support internal communication, websites, and documentation across the college. The team I worked with focuses on helping faculty use these tools effectively, maintaining intranet content, and supporting onboarding for new staff. Working in this environment allowed me to see firsthand how computing solutions support day-to-day academic operations for users with varying levels of technical experience.

Goals

  • Develop practical proficiency in Microsoft 365 tools such as Excel, Power Automate, Teams, and Forms
  • Support faculty onboarding to the OVC intranet in an effective and user-friendly manner
  • Strengthen troubleshooting and communication skills related to SharePoint and CampusPress support
  • Collaborate effectively as part of a professional support team
  • Develop professional problem-solving, time management, and organizational skills

Job Description

As an OVC Website Training & Support Specialist Co-op Student, I supported faculty and staff at the University of Guelph with website-related tools used across the Ontario Veterinary College. This included troubleshooting issues in SharePoint and CampusPress, maintaining intranet content, and assisting with faculty onboarding.

A large part of my role involved creating, maintaining, and fixing Power Automate flows used for internal OVC processes. When flows failed, I reviewed them step by step to locate errors and restore functionality. In some cases, I met directly with OVC staff, including the accounting team, to design new automations based on their workflow needs. One project involved creating a flow that converted BMP images of shipment slips into HTML files so the contents could be searched, since BMP images could not be indexed.

Alongside automation work, I handled smaller support tasks such as helping professors with CourseLink (D2L) issues and responding to general support requests. This role required balancing technical problem-solving with clear communication, especially when working with users who did not have technical backgrounds. Most of my learning came from handling real support cases and working through problems with my team, rather than from prior coursework alone.

Conclusions

This work term at the Ontario Veterinary College gave me practical experience supporting real users in a professional environment. I worked with enterprise tools such as SharePoint and Power Automate, gained experience troubleshooting live systems, and became more comfortable handling support requests independently.

Compared to the start of the term, I am now more confident identifying issues, fixing them without step-by-step guidance, and communicating solutions clearly to faculty and staff. If someone were asked to describe this website, I would want them to see clear evidence of hands-on experience, skill development, and an improved understanding of how computing support functions in a large academic institution.
